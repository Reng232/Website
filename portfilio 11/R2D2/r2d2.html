R2D2

The project aims to create a detailed replica of R2-D2's head from "Star Wars," focusing on both visual and operational elements. Utilizing the Creality CR 10 S5, the team 3D printed the dome, measuring approximately 450 millimeters (1.48 ft) in diameter. Efforts will include enabling head rotation with servo motors, incorporating speakers for authentic sound effects, and adding LEDs to mimic R2-D2's distinctive blinking and glowing red LED. Programming these features with an Arduino, the project aspires to closely replicate the original robot's functionality and appearance.

Who We Are

The project team members, Angelia and Marlia, are dedicated to creating the R2-D2 replica. Angelia, an avid "Star Wars" enthusiast who has watched all the films, found inspiration in the iconic design and functionality of R2-D2. Marlia, equally passionate about robotics and innovative design, shares this vision. Their combined expertise and enthusiasm drive their commitment to accurately replicating R2-D2's visual and operational aspects, ensuring the project embodies the spirit and precision of the original character.

Key Features

The project incorporates advanced control features, allowing users to manipulate various functions of the R2-D2 replica. Sound control is managed through the controller, providing users with the ability to play and adjust sound effects seamlessly. Additionally, the head can be rotated using the left and right buttons, enhancing the interactive experience. A pivotal component in achieving these functionalities is the ESP32 microcontroller, which plays a crucial role in programming and integrating the various operational aspects of the replica. This technology ensures precise control and enhances the overall authenticity of the R2-D2 model.

Materials

The materials that were used in this project were the following:

  • Arduino Ide
  • L298N motor driver module
  • regular breadboard
  • Esp 32
  • Voice Playback module
  • 3 powersupply
  • Speaker

Obstacles Faced

The project encountered several obstacles, starting with the lack of an available laptop, which delayed progress until a suitable device was found.
The school's infrastructure also presented challenges, complicating various aspects of the work.
Additionally, managing the numerous cables proved difficult, as the tangled mess hindered efficient progress and required significant effort to organize.
Despite these issues, the team's perseverance and resourcefulness allowed them to overcome these challenges and continue advancing the project.
